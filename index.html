Compliance Complexity Summary

Ranking of Material Groups by Regulatory Burden (Avg. Score across countries):

1. Hazardous Additives (Lead, BFRs) – Avg. 2.6/3.0. Highest regulatory burden. Nearly all jurisdictions strictly regulate lead and brominated flame retardants in the IT waste stream. These substances trigger hazardous waste laws (handling and transport permits) in most regions.

2. Battery Metals (Li, Co, etc.) – Avg. 2.5/3.0. Spent batteries face comprehensive regulation. Producer responsibility schemes are widespread – e.g. EU Battery Directive, US state laws on lead-acid batteries, Singapore's new battery EPR – enforcing free collection and recycling.

3. Precious Metals (Au, Ag, Pd) – Avg. 2.4/3.0. Precious metals themselves are not toxic, but in e-waste they reside in complex assemblies (PCBs, connectors) that fall under e-waste regulations.

4. Plastics & Composites (ABS plastics, FR-4 laminates) – Avg. 2.3/3.0. This group is regulated chiefly when the plastics are part of electronic products or contain persistent toxins.

5. Non-Ferrous Metals (Cu, Al, Ni, Sn, Zn) – Avg. 1.7/3.0. Regulation here is moderate. These metals are valuable and non-toxic in elemental form, so pure scrap is less regulated.

6. Ferrous Metals (Steel, iron alloys) – Avg. 1.6/3.0. This group has the lowest burden. Steel racks, cases, and chassis are widely recycled and often exempt from hazardous waste rules.
